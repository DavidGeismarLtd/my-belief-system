# User Personas & Use Cases

## Primary Personas

### Persona 1: The Thoughtful Voter

**Name**: Sarah, 34, Software Engineer

**Background**:
- Votes in every election (local, national)
- Reads news regularly but feels overwhelmed
- Has strong values but struggles to articulate them
- Frustrated by "lesser of two evils" framing
- Wants to make informed decisions, not tribal ones

**Goals**:
- Understand which candidates actually align with her values
- Track whether politicians keep their promises
- Feel confident in her voting decisions
- Reduce time spent consuming political news

**Pain Points**:
- "I don't know if I'm a Democrat or Republican anymore"
- "Politicians say one thing and do another"
- "I can't remember what candidates stood for last election"
- "I feel like I'm voting based on personality, not policy"

**How First Principles Helps**:
- Creates a clear value portrait she can reference
- Tracks politician positions over time
- Shows alignment based on principles, not party labels
- Reduces decision anxiety with structured analysis

**Key Features**:
- Value portrait builder
- Alignment dashboard
- Historical tracking
- Evidence view

---

### Persona 2: The Politically Curious

**Name**: Marcus, 27, Teacher

**Background**:
- Relatively new to active political engagement
- Wants to understand politics beyond social media
- Feels pressure to "pick a side" but resists labels
- Interested in learning, not just confirming biases

**Goals**:
- Understand his own political values
- Learn how political parties differ on principles
- Avoid getting sucked into partisan echo chambers
- Make sense of political complexity

**Pain Points**:
- "I don't know where I fit politically"
- "Everyone says I should be [party], but I'm not sure"
- "Political quizzes give me a label, not understanding"
- "I want to learn, not be told what to think"

**How First Principles Helps**:
- Helps him discover and articulate his values
- Shows political landscape without partisan framing
- Explains differences in terms of principles
- Provides educational context, not just scores

**Key Features**:
- Value discovery onboarding
- Multi-party comparison view
- Explanatory content
- Value dimension definitions

---

### Persona 3: The Disillusioned Partisan

**Name**: Robert, 52, Small Business Owner

**Background**:
- Lifelong [party] voter
- Feels his party has changed or left him behind
- Loyal but increasingly uncomfortable
- Wants to understand if it's him or the party that changed

**Goals**:
- Understand if his values still align with his party
- See how party positions have shifted over time
- Make a decision about continued support
- Feel okay about potentially changing allegiance

**Pain Points**:
- "I don't recognize my party anymore"
- "Am I the one who changed, or did they?"
- "I feel disloyal even questioning this"
- "I don't want to switch tribes, I want to follow my values"

**How First Principles Helps**:
- Shows his stable value portrait over time
- Tracks how party positions have shifted
- Frames decision as value-alignment, not betrayal
- Provides evidence for changes he's sensing

**Key Features**:
- Historical drift detection
- Party evolution timeline
- Value stability tracking
- Contradiction highlighting

---

### Persona 4: The Policy Wonk

**Name**: Jennifer, 41, Policy Analyst

**Background**:
- Deep knowledge of political issues
- Frustrated by superficial political coverage
- Wants granular, evidence-based analysis
- Interested in cross-party policy comparison

**Goals**:
- Track politician positions at detailed level
- Compare policy stances across value dimensions
- Access source material and evidence
- Understand nuanced differences

**Pain Points**:
- "Most political tools are too simplistic"
- "I want to see the actual voting records, not summaries"
- "Single-issue scorecards miss the complexity"
- "I need more than 'left vs right'"

**How First Principles Helps**:
- Provides multidimensional value framework
- Links to source material and evidence
- Allows deep-dive into specific value areas
- Tracks detailed position changes

**Key Features**:
- Evidence view with sources
- Detailed value breakdowns
- Policy-to-value mapping
- Advanced filtering and comparison

**Key Features**: Streamlined onboarding, top-line alignment view, election-time notifications

## Anti-Personas

Who we are **NOT** building for:

### Anti-Persona 1: The Partisan Warrior
- Wants ammunition for political arguments
- Seeks confirmation of existing tribal loyalties
- Not interested in nuance or uncertainty
- **Why not**: Product is designed for clarity, not combat

### Anti-Persona 2: The Politically Apathetic
- No interest in politics or voting
- Won't invest time in value discovery
- Doesn't care about alignment
- **Why not**: Product requires baseline political interest

### Anti-Persona 3: The Conspiracy Theorist
- Distrusts all institutions and data
- Seeks validation of fringe theories
- Not interested in evidence-based analysis
- **Why not**: Product relies on verifiable sources and structured reasoning

## Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Pre-Election Decision Making

**Actor**: Thoughtful Voter (Sarah)

**Scenario**: Local election in 3 months, 4 candidates running

**Flow**:
1. Sarah opens First Principles 3 months before election
2. Reviews her value portrait (created 6 months ago)
3. Sees alignment scores for all 4 candidates
4. Drills into specific value dimensions where candidates differ
5. Reviews evidence for each candidate's positions
6. Tracks candidates over the 3-month campaign period
7. Makes voting decision based on sustained alignment

**Success Criteria**: Sarah feels confident in her choice and can articulate why

---

### Use Case 2: Value Discovery

**Actor**: Politically Curious (Marcus)

**Scenario**: First-time user wants to understand his political values

**Flow**:
1. Marcus signs up and starts onboarding
2. Answers value trade-off questions (15-20 minutes)
3. Reviews his generated value portrait
4. Reads explanations for each value dimension
5. Refines portrait by adjusting specific values
6. Explores how his values map to political parties
7. Saves portrait and sets up monitoring

**Success Criteria**: Marcus says "I understand my values better now"

---

### Use Case 3: Detecting Party Drift

**Actor**: Disillusioned Partisan (Robert)

**Scenario**: Long-time party supporter questioning alignment

**Flow**:
1. Robert creates value portrait
2. Sees current alignment with his party (lower than expected)
3. Views historical alignment timeline (shows decline over 5 years)
4. Explores specific value dimensions where drift occurred
5. Reviews evidence of party position changes
6. Compares with other parties to see alternatives
7. Makes informed decision about continued support

**Success Criteria**: Robert understands what changed and feels okay with his decision

---

### Use Case 4: Deep Policy Analysis

**Actor**: Policy Wonk (Jennifer)

**Scenario**: Comparing two politicians on specific policy area

**Flow**:
1. Jennifer navigates to comparison view
2. Selects two politicians and specific value dimension (e.g., "economic equality")
3. Reviews detailed breakdown of positions
4. Clicks through to source material (voting records, speeches)
5. Sees timeline of position changes
6. Exports data for her own analysis

**Success Criteria**: Jennifer finds the analysis rigorous and well-sourced

---

### Use Case 5: Monitoring Over Time

**Actor**: Thoughtful Voter (Sarah)

**Scenario**: Checking in quarterly to track alignment changes

**Flow**:
1. Sarah opens app (hasn't checked in 3 months)
2. Sees notification: "2 significant changes in your alignment"
3. Reviews change summary
4. Drills into specific changes (e.g., politician shifted on climate policy)
5. Reads evidence and context
6. Updates her mental model of that politician
7. Continues monitoring

**Success Criteria**: Sarah stays informed without daily news consumption

## User Journey Map

### Stage 1: Discovery
- **Touchpoint**: Hears about First Principles from friend/article
- **Emotion**: Curious, skeptical
- **Goal**: Understand what this is
- **Action**: Visits landing page

### Stage 2: Onboarding
- **Touchpoint**: Value portrait builder
- **Emotion**: Engaged, thoughtful
- **Goal**: Create accurate value portrait
- **Action**: Answers questions, reviews results

### Stage 3: Initial Exploration
- **Touchpoint**: Alignment dashboard
- **Emotion**: Surprised, validated, or challenged
- **Goal**: Understand alignment with known actors
- **Action**: Explores different parties/politicians

### Stage 4: Deep Dive
- **Touchpoint**: Evidence views, timelines
- **Emotion**: Analytical, curious
- **Goal**: Understand *why* alignment exists
- **Action**: Reviews sources, tracks changes

### Stage 5: Ongoing Monitoring
- **Touchpoint**: Periodic check-ins
- **Emotion**: Informed, confident
- **Goal**: Stay updated on alignment changes
- **Action**: Reviews updates, refines portrait

### Stage 6: Decision Making
- **Touchpoint**: Pre-election review
- **Emotion**: Confident, clear
- **Goal**: Make informed voting decision
- **Action**: Final alignment check, votes
